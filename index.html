Distribution of Banking Products & Services

A Case Study of ICICI Bank

Project Report for Sales Distribution and Retail Management

Submitted by: Group J

Mohit Agarwal, Yashika Gupta, Akshita Jain, Pranjal Sukhija

Project Objectives

  • Analyse the main distribution channels for ICICI products.
  • Examine the transformation of physical vs. digital channels.
  • Study customer segmentation across urban and rural markets.
  • Assess technology adoption and its impact on service delivery.
  • Identify challenges, opportunities, and improvements.

Methodology

  • Conducted interviews with key bank employees.
  • Reviewed available data on product distribution channels.
  • Studied internal and published materials on product adoption.
  • Insights gathered from an industry expert, Mr. Mayank Singh, Regional Head Sales at ICICI Bank.

Channel Analysis

Branch Channel

The backbone of distribution. Physical branches for direct, personalized customer service, operated by salaried employees.

Builder/DRO Channel

Partnerships with property builders (e.g., DLF, M3M) to streamline mortgage products for new projects.

DSA/DMM Vertical

External agents, brokers, and CAs who distribute products, primarily loans, on a commission basis.

Channel Comparison

Channel Partners Most Used For Cost Effectiveness
Branch Salaried employees All products, routine needs Highest
Builder/DRO Builders, DRO team Mortgages for new projects Moderate
DSA/DMM Agents, CAs, brokers Loans, alternative cases Lowest

The Branch Channel is most dominant due to its cost-effectiveness and central management, serving as the preferred choice for both the bank and its customers.

Product Distribution & Digital Adoption

Mortgages (Home Loans)

Traditionally branch-driven via RMs and DSAs. Now increasingly uses the iLens digital platform for faster, paperless approvals. Pre-approved offers are also pushed through the iMobile Pay app.

Personal Loans

A digital-first product. Instant loans with e-KYC and Aadhaar-based e-sign through iMobile Pay and the website are common. Branch channels cater to non-pre-approved customers.

Credit Cards

Acquired through both digital campaigns (website, app, e-commerce tie-ups) and physical channels (DSAs, in-branch). Instant virtual cards are issued for partnerships like the Amazon Pay–ICICI card.

Fixed Deposits (FDs)

One of the most digitized products. Over 80% of new deposits are opened via iMobile Pay and Internet Banking. Branches remain crucial for senior citizens and rural customers.

Insurance

Primarily sold via bancassurance and branch channels, supported by relationship managers. Digital journeys on the app and website are increasingly used for faster policy issuance.

Customer Segmentation & Financial Inclusion

Urban vs. Rural Markets

Urban Customers

Fast-paced, consumer-driven needs. Higher demand for credit cards, auto loans, and home loans to manage a modern lifestyle.

Rural Customers

Needs are tied to community, farming, and family. High demand for gold loans, education loans, and business loans for local shops.

Salaried vs. Self-Employed

Salaried Individuals

Stable, predictable income. Targeted with products for personal spending like credit cards and auto loans.

Self-Employed Individuals

Variable income stream. Targeted with products to support business growth, such as business loans and commercial property loans.

Financial Inclusion Challenge

A key challenge is bridging the urban-rural divide. In cities, a branch may be available every 5 km, but in rural areas, a single branch may serve 5-10 villages. Bank staff act as community educators to build trust and increase awareness, but significant work remains to make banking truly accessible for all.

Technology, Challenges & Recommendations

Technology & Innovation

  • Advanced CRM for lead tracking.
  • Proprietary 'iLens' platform.
  • AI-based chatbots (iPal).
  • Predictive analytics for offers.
  • Mobile apps for sales force.

Key Challenges

  • Low digital literacy in rural areas.
  • Channel conflicts and overlaps.
  • Complex digital journeys for some products.
  • Data privacy and compliance risks.
  • Uneven sales force productivity.

Recommendations

  • Invest in digital literacy campaigns.
  • Develop vernacular apps for rural areas.
  • Create a 360-degree customer view.
  • Empower channel partners with digital training.
  • Evolve into a balanced 'Phygital' ecosystem.
